## 🎯 PHASE 0 OUTPUTS AT A GLANCE

### 1. PRODUCT DEFINITION

**Vision:** 
*FST Pay is an AI-powered digital wallet and virtual card platform that empowers teenagers and young adults to develop healthy financial habits through intelligent expense tracking, AI-driven budgeting, and gamified financial education.*

**Target Users:**
- 🎓 **Primary:** Teenagers (13-18) with pocket money
- 👨‍🎓 **Secondary:** College students (18-22) with part-time income
- 💼 **Tertiary:** Young professionals (22-25) earning first salary
- 👨‍👩‍👦 **Stakeholder:** Parents wanting financial visibility

**Market Opportunity:**
- TAM: 450M teenagers in India
- SAM (2-year): 25M youth in metro/tier-2 cities
- SOM (Year 1): 100K-1M users
- Market: NASCENT (no direct competitor in India)

## 🎓 KEY DECISIONS MADE IN PHASE 0

### Decision 1: FREE Model
**Rationale:** India market has low willingness-to-pay for subscription fintech. FREE removes adoption friction, enables rapid user growth, monetizes via partnerships later.

**Alternative Considered:** Freemium (₹99/month for premium). Rejected: would reduce teen adoption.

---

### Decision 2: Teen Autonomy vs Parental Control
**Rationale:** Teenage users want independence, not surveillance. Parents want visibility, not control. Optional parental mode preserves both.

**Alternative Considered:** Mandatory parental controls (like Greenlight). Rejected: would frustrate teens.

---

### Decision 3: AI Coach as Key Differentiator
**Rationale:** Competitors offer static financial literacy content. AI coach provides personalized, real-time guidance = better engagement + retention.

**Alternative Considered:** No AI (MVP simpler). Rejected: loses key differentiator.

---

### Decision 4: India-First Product Design
**Rationale:** Indian payment flows (UPI, bank transfers) are different from Western (cards, ACH). Design for India first, then expand.

**Alternative Considered:** Adapt Western product to India. Rejected: too many friction points.

---

### Decision 5: Modular Monolith Architecture
**Rationale:** Monolith is simpler to deploy at MVP stage. Modularity allows future microservices. Avoids overengineering.

**Alternative Considered:** Microservices from day 1. Rejected: too much complexity for MVP.
